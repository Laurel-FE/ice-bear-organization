Frequently asked questions

Are polar bears endangered?


Polar bears are classified differently based on international, national, and regional regulations. The IUCN lists them as a vulnerable species on a global scale.

How many polar bears are there?


Scientists can only give informed estimations. According to the most recent IUCN study, there are roughly 26,000 of them. According to a more recent research (Hamilton & Derocher, 2018), there are around 23,000.

What is global warming?


The increases in average global temperature since the Industrial Revolution is referred to as global warming. Since 1880, the average global temperature has risen by around one degree Celsius (1.8 degrees Fahrenheit). Global warming is a continuous process; scientists predict that the average global temperature will rise by 0.3 to 0.7 degrees Celsius (0.54-1.26 degrees Fahrenheit) by 2035.

Does global warming affect polar bears?


The Arctic is warming about twice as fast as the global average, causing the ice that polar bears depend on to melt away.
